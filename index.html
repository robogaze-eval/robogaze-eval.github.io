RoboGaze: Evaluating Robot World Models via Structured Vision-Language Analysis

A training-free, multi-agent VLM evaluator that turns generated robot-manipulation videos into structured, temporally localized diagnostic reports — telling you what failed, when, why, and how severely.

Abstract

Recent advances in robot world models enable synthetic video generation for embodied prediction and planning. However, evaluating these videos is challenging: visually realistic outputs often violate physical laws, temporal consistency, or task logic, while conventional metrics and monolithic Vision-Language Model (VLM) judges fail to generalize or provide precise diagnostic value.

We present RoboGaze, a training-free, multi-agent VLM framework that provides structured, interpretable evaluation for generated robot-manipulation videos. Given a task instruction and video, RoboGaze operates via a three-stage pipeline: task-scene grounding, dimension-specific specialist routing, and critic-based verification. It outputs temporally localized glitch reports categorized under a novel 6-dimension, 30-type robotics-specific taxonomy.

To benchmark RoboGaze, we introduce a human-validated dataset of 382 clips spanning simulated and real-world multi-view manipulation. Evaluating eight open-source and proprietary VLM backbones, RoboGaze dramatically outperforms zero-shot baselines, improving description-F1 by up to +43 points and temporal alignment (F1 × IoU) by up to +37 points — closing approximately 85% of the gap to the human ceiling. Its critic verifier mitigates the “cry-wolf” false-positive flaw of standard VLMs, lifting clean-clip accuracy from under 25% to over 80%.

Three Key Ideas

RoboGaze reframes robot-video evaluation as a structured reasoning problem rather than a single quality score. Three ingredients make it work.

Robotics-specific glitch taxonomy

A hierarchical 6×30 taxonomy of robot-video failures spanning task execution, instruction consistency, object interactions, robot behavior, physical plausibility, and visual quality — replacing one opaque score with named, actionable failure types.

6 dimensions · 30 failure types

Subtask-level temporal grounding

Instead of localizing over the full video, RoboGaze converts localization into clip-level task-progress reasoning and groups clips into subtask segments — pinpointing when each failure happens during execution.

+37 points temporal alignment

Verifier-based multi-agent reasoning

A critic verifier re-examines every specialist hypothesis against visual evidence, task context, and scene state — accepting, rejecting, or merging it. This contests false alarms before they enter the report.

Eliminates “cry-wolf” false positives

The RoboGaze Framework

A three-phase pipeline turns raw multimodal inputs into a verified, structured glitch report.

  1. Phase 1 Input & Context Grounding. Parse the task instruction and initial frame into structured task memory and scene memory, and decompose the video into clip-level execution observations grouped by subtask.
  2. Phase 2 Candidate Discovery & Specialist Analysis. A router predicts which glitch dimensions are plausible per subtask, then dimension-specific specialists produce evidence-grounded, temporally localized hypotheses.
  3. Phase 3 Verification & Reporting. A critic verifier validates, rejects, or merges each hypothesis, then synthesizes the survivors into a single coherent report with dimensions, timestamps, evidence, and severity.

A Robotics-Specific Glitch Taxonomy

Failures are organized along six coarse dimensions, each refined into fine-grained types — 30 in total — spanning the full execution stack of a manipulation rollout.

1Task ProgressSubtask completion and execution order
2Instruction ConsistencyAlignment with the commanded task
3Object–SceneObject existence, identity, and interactions
4Robot-BodyRobot kinematics and self-consistency
5Physical PlausibilityContacts, collisions, and dynamics
6Visual QualityRendering, artifacts, and temporal coherence

Specialist agents reason at the fine-grained type level, while headline results aggregate at the dimension level — giving both pinpoint diagnoses and an interpretable summary.
